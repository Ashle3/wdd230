Frisco Heritage Museum

The Frisco Heritage Museum is a beloved staple of the city, and is informative to all who enters its doors. Opened in 2008, the museum offers hisorical facts on the beginnings of the city and its rich history. It is a place that is highly recommended by those who live here.

Frisco Heritage Village

The Frisco Heritage Village is a set of grounds just outside of the Heritage Museum. This peaceful spot in downtown Frisco provides visitors with the opportunity to explore actual historical sites that were in the beginning days pf Frisco, including houses, a schoolhouse, and a train depot.

Frisco Train Museum

One thing Frisco is known for, is its rich history as a rail town. The train museum celebrates this history, with outdoor exhibits of trains of various models, and different time periods. It is highly recommended for train lovers of all kinds.

Frisco Square

Frisco Square serves as the center of downtown Frisco, and is a popular tourist spot for all. With city hall as the main backdrop, the square is filled with shops, movie theaters, restaurants, and apartments. During the holiday season, the square is transformed and decorated with lights that dance to pre-set music; it truly is a spectacular sight to see!

The Star

Also known as the Ford Center, the Star serves as the headquarters and practice center for the Dallas Cowboys. Open to the public, the Star is also used to host High School football games, and other special events. Come see the Cowboys during the last week of August, when they open their practices to the public!
